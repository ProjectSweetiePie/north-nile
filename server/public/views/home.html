Nile of the North

Map Log In

North Minneapolis is not unlike many other marginalized urban communities of color that have in recent times come to be coined “food deserts”. Food deserts can be described as geographic areas where resident’s access to affordable healthy food options (especially fresh fruits and vegetables) is restricted.

Nile of the North is a green vision of economic and ecological vitality. It has evolved from the diligent collaboration of a cohort of North Minneapolis residents, citizens interested in food justice, non-profits, social service agencies, and businesses. These food champions have been intent on creating a social, sustainable healthy food system. Their ongoing efforts to eradicate food insecurity stand in stark contrast to the local media portrayal and perception of the North Side as a community plagued by crime, death, and vigilance.
