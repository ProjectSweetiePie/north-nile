Without vision the people shall perish... Proverbs 29:18

The “Nile of the North” is a green vision of economic and ecological vitality. It has evolved from and been inspired by the diligent and collaborative hard work of a cohort of north side Minneapolis residents, concerned citizens interested in food justice, non- profits, social service agencies, and businesses intent on creating a social, sustainable healthy food system. These food champions and their ongoing efforts to eradicate food insecurity stand in stark contrast to the local media portrayal and perception of the north-side as a community plagued by crime, death, and vigilance.

North Minneapolis not unlike many other marginalized urban communities of color have in recent times come to be coined “food deserts”. Food deserts can be described as geographic areas where resident’s access to affordable healthy food options (especially fresh fruits and vegetables) is restricted. The Northside Fresh Coalition was established in 2010, and facilitated by the Northpoint Health and Wellness Center, Inc. The goal was to convene organizations working on food related health initiatives to create a shared vision for North Minneapolis’s food system and community. Improving our food system is key to overcoming economic, environmental, and social barriers to health and the revitalization of our community. “The Nile of the North” was informed by, inspired by, and borne of this collective effort. It is a living testimony to our shared humanity.

North Minneapolis is a community rich in ethnic diversity, rich in history. It is a community that is rich in culture. The “Nile of the North” becomes the faceplate, the shared reservoir, the community showcase that displays that richness. We are creating a trade route, a 21st century pathway to prosperity. Welcome to the Village of North Minneapolis/Home of Northside Fresh.
